Project 01: Seattle PD Funding Eligibility

The analysis aims to gain insight about the logs of emergency 911 calls from the Seattle Police Department reported on March 26, 2016 - March 28, 2016. In addition, the study discusses whether the department will be eligible for additional funding if the minimum standard of 2.5 officers onsite per incident is met.

BUSINESS PROBLEM: If not eligible, how can the police department increase their chances of eligibility for funding?
SOLUTION: The overall mean of the data is 1.889 which is below the 2.5 threshold and is not qualified for additional funding. In order to increase their eligibility for funding, the Seattle PD must focus on zones with average officers at scene that is less than or equal to 2 and the largest reported incident zones. There are 12 zones that need assistance and can lead to funding eligibility.

Project 02: Customer Churn Analysis & Predictive Modeling

The study analyzes customer data from a telecommunications company to identify why customers are leaving and the potential indicators that are causing them to leave. The three predictive modeling techniques that will be used in this analysis are (1) Binary Logistic Regression (2) Decision trees and (3) Random forest.

BUSINESS PROBLEM: What factors are causing churn? How can the company reduce churn rate with present and future customers?
SOLUTION: The winning model is the random forest algorithm with an overall accuracy of 80.21% and AUC of 81.50%. This means that the model will correctly predict churn 80.21% of the time and there is a 81.50% chance that the model can distinguish between positive (churn) and negative (no churn) classes. In order for the telecommunications company to reduce the current churn rate of 26.58%, the company should focus on contracts (month-to-month specifically), tenure length, and total charges to start. The company should give incentives such as reduced pricing or discounted extra services for long-term customers to keep them from leaving to another competitor.

Project 04: California Population Prediction

Linear regression analysis on California population and predicting population size for the next 5 years using the United States Census Bureau data.

BUSINESS PROBLEM: Since California is a populous state, predict the population for the next 10 years. In addition, is the data set flawed for prediction?
SOLUTION: The linear equation is Population = 37,198,671 + 258,094(year). This means that a unit change in Year will increase by 258,094 for the California population. By 2030, the predicted population for the state of California is 42,618,653 reported citizens. However, there is a flaw in the data set because there may be citizens who have not reported their status living in the state. Some reasons can include that the citizens did not receive the reminder mail, forgot to file, or choose not to despite the fine. Additionally, the data can be slightly more accurate if more years were provided by the US Census Bureau since the prediction is based on the last 10 years.

Capstone Project: EDA & Hotel Cancellation Predictive Modeling

The study explores hotel data from the southern part of Portugal and identify guest cancellation predictors for business decisions. In addition, the following study attempts to create a predictive model (K-nearest neighbors, logistic regression, and random forest) and estimate cancelled bookings.

BUSINESS PROBLEM: What factors are causing cancelled hotel bookings? How can the Portugal hotels reduce their cancellation rate?
SOLUTION: The winning model is the random forest algorithm with an overall accuracy of 84.50% and precision of 87.43%. This means that the model will correctly predict hotel cancellation 84.50% of the time. In order for hotel management company to reduce their current hotel cancellation rate of ~37%, management should focus on requiring deposits because 80% of the data require no deposits. This can be mitigated if hotels require fees for cancellation or mandatory deposits. When hotels have stricter cancellation policies, guests are less inclined to cancel their reservation and hotel revenue will increase. In addition, lead time is another factor to be targeted. Guests who hold reservations for long periods of time are more likley to cancel. If management offers special offers or larger discount for on-site services when booking in advance, guests are less likely to cancel.
